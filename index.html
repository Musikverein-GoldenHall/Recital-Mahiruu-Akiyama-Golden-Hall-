About

From Kyoto To Vienna

The Sound Of My Grandmother Koto — That's Where Everything Started

Born In Kyoto 2007 , Mahiruu Akiyama Lived Near to Temples , In a house where music was played everyday.

After playing in Kyoto conservatory . She Had a Scholarship that made her go to Paris , Then Metz in 2020. Thats where she played 5 year on public place and in conservatory .

There is in Mahiruu Akiyama's playing something impossible to fully name. A quality of presence, perhaps. A relationship to silence that comes from far away — from Nara, from the mornings she watched the deer cross the mist before the temples.

"The silence of Nara taught me that the space between sounds is a note like any other."

Her Chopin unsettles habits in the best sense. No easy pathos, no virtuosity for its own sake. She digs, seeks the bone beneath the flesh, finds the precise melancholy where others find the general. Critics speak of a "rare interiority", a playing "heard from within".
